L2-estimates for the evolving surface finite element method

  • Authors: Dziuk & Elliott

Main contribution

  • optimal and uniform in time \(L^2\) bound for linear fem for advection and diffusion equation on a moving surface

Main Existing results

  • \(H^1\) optimal error in [9], \(L^\infty L^2\) and \(L^2H^1\) in \(\mathcal{O}(h)\). \begin{align} \sup _{t \in(0, T)}\left\|u(\cdot, t)-u_h(\cdot, t)\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma(t))}^2+\int_0^T\left\|\nabla_{\Gamma}\left(u(\cdot, t)-u_h(\cdot, t)\right)\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma(t))}^2 d t \leq c h^2 \end{align}

Method

  • Weak solution \begin{align} \int_{\Gamma(t)} \partial^{\bullet} u \varphi+\int_{\Gamma(t)} u \varphi \nabla_{\Gamma} \cdot v+\int_{\Gamma(t)} \mathcal{A} \nabla_{\Gamma} u \cdot \nabla_{\Gamma} \varphi=0 \end{align} for \(\varphi(\cdot, t) \in H^1(\Gamma(t))\) where \(\mathcal{A}\) is positive definite on the tangential space \begin{align} \mathcal{A} \xi \cdot \xi \geq c_0|\xi|^2 \quad \forall \xi \in \mathbb{R}^{n+1}, \xi \cdot \nu=0 \end{align}

  • For initial data \(u(0)\in H^1\), and \(v\in W^{1,\infty}(\mathcal{G}_T)\), the existence and uniqueness of the above weak solution is proved in DE2007IJNA following the Galerkin method. Precisely, proving the bounededness of \(u_N\) in \(H^1\) and taking the weak convergence subsequence.

  • Stability: For initial data \(u(0)\in H^1\), velocity \(v\in C^1(\mathcal{G}_T)\) \begin{align} \begin{aligned} & \sup _{(0, T)}\|u\|_{L^2(\Gamma)}^2+\int_0^T\left\|\nabla_{\Gamma} u\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma)}^2 \leq c\left(\mathcal{A}, v, \mathcal{G}_T, T\right)\left\|u_0\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma(0))}^2 \\ & \int_0^T\left\|\partial^{\bullet} u\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma)}^2+\sup _{(0, T)}\left\|\nabla_{\Gamma} u\right\|_{L^2(\Gamma)}^2 \leq c\left(\mathcal{A}, v, \mathcal{G}_T, T\right)\left\|u_0\right\|_{H^1(\Gamma(0))}^2 \\ \end{aligned} \end{align}

  • Scheme by setting the material derivative onto the test function (Evolving Surface Finite Element Method).

Given \(U_{h 0} \in S_h(0)\) find \(U_h \in S_h^T=\left\{\phi_h\right.\) and \(\left.\partial_h^{\circ} \phi_h \in C^0\left(\mathcal{G}_T^h\right) \mid \phi_h(\cdot, t) \in S_h(t),\ t \in[0, T]\right\}\) such that for all \(\phi_h \in S_h^T\) and \(t \in(0, T]\)

\[\frac{d}{d t} m_h\left(U_h, \phi_h\right)+a_h\left(U_h, \phi_h\right)=m_h\left(U_h, \partial_h^{\circ} \phi_h\right), \quad U_h(\cdot, 0)=U_{h 0}\]

Using the transport property of basis function, it follows that this definition is equivalent to:

\[\frac{d}{d t} m_h\left(U_h, \chi_j\right)+a_h\left(U_h, \chi_j\right)=0 \quad U_h(\cdot, 0)=U_{h 0}\]

for all \(j=1, \ldots, N\).

\begin{align} & a_h\left(\phi_h, W_h\right)=\int_{\Gamma_h} \mathcal{A}^{-l} \nabla_{\Gamma_h} \phi_h \cdot \nabla_{\Gamma_h} W_h, \\ & m_h\left(\phi_h, W_h\right)=\int_{\Gamma_h} \phi_h W_h, \end{align}

Finite element scheme does not explicitly involve the velocity of the surface, but relies on the poisition of surface at t.

Then error will be estimated comparing \begin{align} \frac{d}{d t} \int_{\Gamma(t)} u \varphi+\int_{\Gamma(t)} \mathcal{A} \nabla_{\Gamma} u \cdot \nabla_{\Gamma} \varphi=\int_{\Gamma(t)} u \partial^{\bullet} \varphi \end{align}

Compared with DE2007IJNA, the main difference is the reformulation of the scheme such that the velocity is not explictly involved.

There are two key points: * To compare the numerical solution with the Ritz projeciton, so as to split the error * material derivative of error of Ritz projection, by defining a lifted velocity on \(\Gamma\) via lifted trajectory

Definition 4.3. We define a material velocity for \(x=X(t)\) on the surface \(\Gamma_h(t)\) by (4.5) \(\dot{X}(t)=V_h(X(t), t), V_h(x, t):=\sum_{j=1}^N \dot{X}_j(t) \chi_j(x, t)\) for \(x \in S_h(t)\), and the associated material velocity on \(\Gamma(t)\) by (4.6) \(\quad \dot{Y}(t)=v_h(Y(t), t)=\frac{\partial p}{\partial t}(X(t), t)+V_h(X(t), t) \cdot \nabla p(X(t), t)\) so that for \(x \in \Gamma_h(t)\), (4.7) \(\quad v_h(p(x, t), t)=(P-d \mathcal{H})(x, t) V_h(x, t)-d_t(x, t) \nu(x, t)-d(x, t) \nu_t(x, t)\), where \(Y(t)=p(X(t), t)\) is defined by \((2.2)\).
